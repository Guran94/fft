22. 11. 1980

Why, why, why ... I keep repeating that in my head after yesterday's contact with the rebels. Why did they have to begin with us? Why couldn't the truck go a little faster, or why couldn't we leave the day before yesterday? I was sad and I am scared not only because of Muday. He will not let me be, he always goes after me. I am always the slowest, always I have to do more pushupps than other.
There are very few of us here, but we are the soldiers! They are just the people who took the weapons they didn't shoot in their lives. Civilians are forbidden to leave towns and villages. It's pointless to talk about farms, they are no longer under our control. Meanwhile, we're getting pretty good! I'll get back to that yesterday.

The way we walked through the forest from the rebels yesterday, we can actually be happy. Elsewhere, there were five times as many in the forest and some areas are uncleaned. The sergeant repeated again and again not to get close to the forests, because the rebels are hidding there. I was quite taken by the situation we were not in. I hid in the trees, listening to the shots of my partners and my enemies. I just got to know the war environment.

We couldn't lose the contact accidentely. In my opinion either the rebels striked on it, or our guys have slept during the night, so we're going to have a big bitch, but a lot of the guys in my group are really dire. In fact, me too.


Since yesterday we are at war, soldier. The rebels cought weapons and fortified in forests.
Early this morning we've lost the contact with the ammo depot. Therefore we're sending you to look and find out what actually happened there.



Tasks

1) Get in the truck in Montignac HQ. Driver is going to take you through the control checkopoint to the unexplored area, where communication was cut off with our soldiers.

2) Check ammo depot. Any troubles with the rebels report immediately.

3) Blow the ammunition up and wait until the M113 armored personnel carrier arrives.
